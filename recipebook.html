Steak

  • Time: 20 min
  • Сalorie content: 288 kkal/100 g
Ingredients Quantity
Beef 350 g
Pepper 20g
Spice to taste
Bay leaf 3 g
Garlic 5 g
Salt to taste
Oil 68 g

Step 1: To cook the right beef steak, you must first decide on the degree of roast (there are 5 of them, classic, but many choose Medium Rare, Medium and Medium Well) and choose a piece of meat of the desired thickness.

Step 2: For the steak to turn out right, the meat must be completely dry before cooking - let it get wet.

Step 3: Next, spices. Ground black pepper is traditionally used. It is better to grind it right before the very hot in a convenient way. But keep in mind - not to the state of dust, but to the state of such particles as in the photo.

Step 4: At this point, you can already put the pan on gas - let it heat up, we need a well-heated pan (better - a grill). While the trial is up, salt a piece of meat, greased in front of this oil, roll in freshly crushed pepper, let it lie down, and then gently beat the surface with your fingers so that the salt and spices fix well on it.

Step 5: If the pan is hot, pour oil into it. Warmed up? Immediately send the steak here, overlaid with spices, bay leaves, garlic, cut into slices. The oil should be greasy.

Step 6: We do not leave the stove for a second. We need to catch the right moment, because the degree of roasting should be exactly what you have chosen. For a lightly browned steak, three minutes frying on both sides is enough. If you want a better crust, add more minutes, and so on. When turning over to the other side, never use a knife or fork. The juice should not run out. Therefore, we take something more comfortable and turn it over to the other side.

Step 7: Is the steak ready? Take it out of the pan and serve. The steak is Medium Rare.

Spaghetti bolognese

  • Time: 2 hr and 15 mins
  • Сalorie content: 624 kkal/per serving
Ingredients Quantity
Olive oil 1 tbsp
Smoked streaky bacon 4 rashers
Onion 2 pieces
Carrot 2 pieces
Celery 2 sticks
Garlic 2 cloves
Rosemary 2-3 sprigs
Beef mince 500 g
Tomato 800 g
Oregano 1 tsp
Bay leaf 2 pieces
Tomato purée 2 tbsp
Beef stock 1 cube
Red chilli 1 piece
Red wine 125 ml
Cherry tomatoes 6 pieces
Parmesan 75 g
Spaghetti 400 g

Step 1: Put a large saucepan on a medium heat and add 1 tbsp olive oil.

Step 2: Add 4 finely chopped bacon rashers and fry for 10 mins until golden and crisp.

Step 3: Reduce the heat and add the 2 onions, 2 carrots, 2 celery sticks, 2 garlic cloves and the leaves from 2-3 sprigs rosemary, all finely chopped, then fry for 10 mins. Stir the veg often until it softens.

Step 4: Increase the heat to medium-high, add 500g beef mince and cook stirring for 3-4 mins until the meat is browned all over.

Step 5: Add 2 tins plum tomatoes, the finely chopped leaves from ¾ small pack basil, 1 tsp dried oregano, 2 bay leaves, 2 tbsp tomato purée, 1 beef stock cube, 1 deseeded and finely chopped red chilli (if using), 125ml red wine and 6 halved cherry tomatoes. Stir with a wooden spoon, breaking up the plum tomatoes.

Step 6: Bring to the boil, reduce to a gentle simmer and cover with a lid. Cook for 1 hr 15 mins stirring occasionally, until you have a rich, thick sauce.

Step 7: Add the 75g grated parmesan, check the seasoning and stir.

Step 8: When the bolognese is nearly finished, cook 400g spaghetti following the pack instructions.

Step 9: Drain the spaghetti and either stir into the bolognese sauce, or serve the sauce on top. Serve with more grated parmesan, the remaining basil leaves and crusty bread, if you like.

Mojito

  • Time: 5 min
  • Сalorie content: 34 kkal/per serving
Ingredients Quantity
Sugar 1 tbsp
Mint small bunch
Lime 3 pieces
Soda water 1 cup

Step 1: Muddle the sugar with leaves from the mint using a pestle and mortar (or use a small bowl and the end of a rolling pin).

Step 2: Put a handful of crushed ice into 2 tall glasses. Divide the lime juice between the glasses with the mint mix. Add a straw and top up with soda water.
